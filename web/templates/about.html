How Stock Pattern Prediction Works

Understanding time-based sequence analysis for stock market landmarks

What is Time-Based Sequence Analysis?

Traditional technical analysis focuses on PRICE patterns (head & shoulders, double bottom, etc.). Our approach is different: we analyze TIME patterns.

We ask: WHEN do landmark points (significant lows or highs) tend to occur, measured in week indices from the stock's listing date?

Example:

If a stock has significant low points at weeks 52, 366, 680, 994... we look for mathematical patterns in these numbers to predict the NEXT low point.

How It Works: Step by Step
1
Collect Historical Data

Download weekly OHLCV data from stock's IPO to present.

2
Detect Landmarks

Use ZigZag algorithm (5% threshold) to identify significant price reversals.

3

Convert landmark dates to week numbers from IPO (e.g., week 366).

4

Analyze sequences for arithmetic/geometric patterns (e.g., 52, 366, 680...).

5

Apply pattern to predict next occurrence week number.

6

Translate week number into calendar date range.

Understanding Week Indices

Week indices count the number of weeks from the stock's IPO (initial public offering):

  • Week 0 = First week of trading (IPO week)
  • Week 52 = Approximately 1 year after IPO
  • Week 640 = Approximately 12.3 years after IPO

By using week indices instead of calendar dates, we can discover mathematical patterns that repeat over time, regardless of market conditions, news events, or economic cycles.
